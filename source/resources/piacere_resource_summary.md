## Title and source identity

`Piacere!` is an elementary Italian textbook associated with The University of Iowa and authored by Lucia Gemmani, Irene Lottini, and Claudia Sartini-Rideout.

## What this resource provides

`Piacere!` gives the Workbench a structured beginner curriculum. It is useful when the learner wants to connect class material to a broader course sequence.

Important early sections include:

- `Saluti e presentazioni`;
- `L'alfabeto`;
- `I saluti`;
- `Vocabolario: Presentazioni`;
- numbers from 0 to 100;
- subject pronouns and `avere`;
- `Conversazione: Mi presento`;
- nouns and indefinite articles;
- `essere`, `avere`, and adjectives.

## Best WB use

Use this resource to:

- align first-class slides with a full beginner sequence;

- provide grammar clarification after speech;
- extract vocabulary families into the personal corpus;
- make pronunciation-aware drills;
- create exercises only after the learner has used the phrase.

## Conversation-first transformation

Do not start with a chapter explanation. Start with a speaker need:

```text
I need to greet someone.
I need to say my name.
I need to ask how someone is.
I need to ask what something means.
I need to say where I am from.
```

Then use `Piacere!` as support for accurate phrases, examples, vocabulary, and later clarification.

## High-value first practice path

Ciao.
