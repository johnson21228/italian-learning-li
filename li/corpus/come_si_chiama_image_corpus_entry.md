## Class phrase family

```text
Come si chiama?

Si chiama Micio.
```

## Immediate communicative need

The learner wants to ask a name, give a name, and ask what something is called.

## Situation

Use this when meeting someone, identifying a person, or pointing to an object or animal and asking its name.

## Spoken meaning

```text
Come ti chiami? = What is your name? informal
Come si chiama? = What is his/her name? / What is it called? / What is your name? formal context
Mi chiamo Steve. = My name is Steve.
Si chiama Micio. = It is called Micio. / Its name is Micio.
```

## First-person learner line

```text
Mi chiamo Steve.
```

## Call-and-response practice

```text
A: Ciao. Come ti chiami?
B: Mi chiamo Steve.

A: Come si chiama questo gatto?
B: Si chiama Micio.

A: Come si chiama questo?
B: Non lo so. Come si dice in italiano?
```

## Pronunciation notes

```text
Come = CO-meh
chiama = KYA-ma
Mi chiamo = mee KYA-mo
Si chiama = see KYA-ma
```

The `chi` sound in `chiama` is hard, like `key`, not like English `chip`.

## Repair phrase

```text
Non capisco. Puoi ripetere lentamente?
```

## Grammar later

Only after the learner can hear and answer the phrase, explain lightly:

```text
chiamarsi = to call oneself / to be named
mi chiamo = I am called / my name is
si chiama = he/she/it is called
```
