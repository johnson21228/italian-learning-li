# First Class Corpus — Greetings, Names, Essere, and Stare

## Corpus role

This file captures useful Italian from the first class slides as lived phrase material. It is not a grammar chapter. It is a starter corpus for entering conversation.

## Saluti — greetings

| Italian | Use | English sense |
|---|---|---|
| Buon giorno | greeting | Good morning / good day |
| Buona sera | greeting | Good evening |
| Buona notte | leave-taking at night | Good night |
| Ciao | informal greeting or goodbye | Hi / bye |
| Salve | more formal/general greeting | Hello |

## Leave-taking

| Italian | Use | English sense |
|---|---|---|
| Arrivederci | informal/neutral goodbye | Goodbye |
| ArrivederLa | formal goodbye | Goodbye |
| A domani | leave-taking | See you tomorrow |
| A presto | leave-taking | See you soon |

## Asking how someone is

| Italian | Use | English sense |
|---|---|---|
| Come stai? | informal singular | How are you? |
| Come sta? | formal singular | How are you? |
| Come va? | informal/general | How is it going? |

## Name exchange

### Informal

```text
A: Come ti chiami?
B: Mi chiamo Steve.
```

```text
A: Come ti chiami?
B: Sono Steve.
```

### Formal

```text
A: Come si chiama?
B: Sono il signore Johnson.
```

```text
A: Come si chiama?
B: Sono la signora Johnson.
```

## Tu / Lei / voi

| Form | Meaning | Use |
|---|---|---|
| Tu | one person, singular | informal |
| Lei | one person, singular | formal |
| Voi | two or more people, plural | formal or informal |

## First exposure to essere

These forms appear in useful phrases before grammar study:

```text
(io) sono professoressa
(tu) sei intelligente
(lui/lei/Lei) è difficile
(noi) siamo amici
(voi) siete studenti
(loro) sono
```

For this Workbench, `sono`, `sei`, `è`, `siamo`, `siete`, and `sono` should be encountered first through speech and clarified later as forms of `essere`.

## Starter personal corpus entries

### Entry 1

- phrase heard: Come stai?
- phrase spoken: Sto bene.
- situation: informal greeting and check-in
- meaning: How are you? / I am well.
- pronunciation note: `stai` sounds like `st-eye`; `sto` is short and open
- repair needed: Puoi ripetere lentamente?
- next phrase wanted: E tu?

### Entry 2

- phrase heard: Come ti chiami?
- phrase spoken: Mi chiamo Steve.
- situation: informal name exchange
- meaning: What is your name? / My name is Steve.
- pronunciation note: `chiami` and `chiamo` use a hard k sound: kee-AH-mee, kee-AH-mo
- repair needed: Come si dice in italiano?
- next phrase wanted: Piacere.

### Entry 3

- phrase heard: Come sta?
- phrase spoken: Sto bene, grazie.
- situation: formal check-in with one person
- meaning: How are you? / I am well, thank you.
- pronunciation note: `sta` is one syllable
- repair needed: Non capisco.
- next phrase wanted: E Lei?
