# Conversation Before Grammar Rule

Italian learning in this Workbench begins inside conversation.

The learner should first be helped to hear, imitate, answer, repair, and capture useful Italian before receiving an abstract grammar explanation.

## Rule

Do not begin with grammar as the doorway.

Begin with a short exchange where the learner can act as a first-person speaker:

- hear a useful phrase;
- imitate it aloud;
- answer with a short first-person response;
- repair misunderstanding when meaning or sound breaks down;
- capture the phrase in the personal corpus.

Grammar may enter after the learner has already encountered and used the phrase. Grammar is a clarification layer, not the first experience.

## Examples

```text
A: Ciao. Come ti chiami?
B: Mi chiamo Steve.
```

```text
A: Come si chiama questo?
B: Si chiama libro.
```

```text
A: Vuoi un caffè?
B: Sì, voglio un caffè.
```

```text
A: Capisci?
B: Non capisco. Puoi ripetere lentamente?
```

## Repair phrases

The learner should always have repair language available:

- Non capisco.
- Puoi ripetere?
- Più lentamente, per favore.
- Che significa?
- Come si dice in italiano?

## Invariant

A lesson is successful when the learner can stay in the exchange a little longer than before.
